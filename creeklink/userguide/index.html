MAP


• From the home page click on the “Area of Interest” that is relevant to you eg: East Otway Landcare.

• A Google map will be loaded on which you will see a simple graphic overlay which looks like a segmented snake or snakes running along the named waterways in your Landcare area.

• Each of the segments that you can see relate to a title boundary and will be either blank or will contain one of 4 colours; Green, Light Green, Yellow or Red.

Dark Green occurs where approximately 80% or more of the vegetation is native vegetation exists along waterways

Light Green occurs where there is native vegetation but livestock have not been excluded from the waterway and where there is approximately 80% or more of the vegetation

Yellow occurs where livestock has been excluded from waterways and there is no significant amount of native vegetation

Red occurs where landholders have expressed an interest in undertaking future revegetation works.

(The highlighted / clicked on section is coloured in purple.)

If there is no colour in a given segment then typicallay none of the options listed above apply to that particular segment. (However it is also possible that if a segment is blank then the landholder may have requested that information relating to their land titles not be shown on the web site.

• Users can also click on any segment and obtain additional information relating to that section of waterway. Additional information though clicking on the segment include the name of the creek, the name of the local landcare group, the contact details for that local group, the day vegetation was planted and more.

Please Note: The reliability of information available on a given Landcare area map is the responsibility of the group nominated in the Management Group field within the Details Box. If members of the public have queries or comments relating to specific Landcare areas it is suggested they use the group's contact details which are attached to the group's name.

Landcare Managers are members of local Landcare groups that have been nominated to update the data relevant to their groups.

Landcare Managers are provided with a password which they can use to access the section of the web site where data is updated. The manager's password is accessed from the map page of their relevant group.

LEGEND


EMPTY -

  1. Less than 80% of the vegetation is nantive and live livestock have access to the waterway or
  2. Site has yet to be visited or
  3. Landholder does not wish to have information shown

INTERESTED - Interest in revegetating waterway expressed by Land owner.

LIVESTOCK EXCLUDED - Waterways are not accessible to livestock, typically via fencing work.

NATIVE VEGETATION WITH LIVESTOCK ACCESS - 80% or more of the vegetation is native, live stock has access to waterway.

NATIVE VEGETATION WITH LIVESTOCK EXCLUDED - 80% or more of the vegetation is native, live stock is excluded from the waterway.
